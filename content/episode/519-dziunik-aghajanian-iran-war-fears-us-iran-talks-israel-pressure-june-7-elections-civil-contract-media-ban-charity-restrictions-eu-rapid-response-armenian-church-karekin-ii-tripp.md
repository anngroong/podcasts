This Week in Review covers three major threads, the risk of escalation around Iran and what it means for Armenia’s regional posture, the growing set of legal and political moves shaping Armenia’s June 7 parliamentary elections, and the continuing confrontation between Nikol Pashinyan’s government and the Armenian Church, including actions affecting Catholicos Karekin II and senior clergy.

## Main Topics Addressed

* Iran war fears, the state of US-Iran talks, and Israeli pressure for maximal demands
* Armenia-Iran ties, senior Armenian visits to Tehran, and how TRIPP affects trust
* June 7 parliamentary elections, rule changes, and claims of an uneven playing field
* Opposition landscape and how major blocs are forming ahead of June 7
* Escalating clash between the government and the Armenian Church

## Key Questions Discussed

* How likely is a near-term war on Iran, and what are the red lines in the talks?
* Where do Trump’s instincts differ from Israel and US hardliners on Iran?
* Why the recent stream of senior Armenian visits to Tehran, and what is Iran watching for?
* How has TRIPP changed Iran-Armenia trust and risk perceptions?
* What does the current opposition lineup suggest about June 7 scenarios?
* Is the Church conflict targeted at Karekin II, or aimed at the institution itself?
* Who benefits from a weakened or divided Armenian Church?
