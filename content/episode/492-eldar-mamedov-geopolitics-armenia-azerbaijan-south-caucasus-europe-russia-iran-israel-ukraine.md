This Conversations on Groong episode features **Eldar Mamedov**, former Latvian diplomat, non-resident fellow at the Quincy Institute, and member of the [Pugwash Council on Science & World Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pugwash_Conferences_on_Science_and_World_Affairs).

We discuss rising global tensions following Israel’s war on Iran, Azerbaijan’s expanding geopolitical ambitions, Europe’s unclear foreign policy, and the risks Armenia faces while trying to reposition itself in a turbulent neighborhood.

---

## **Topics Covered**

* Breaking developments around Venezuela and U.S. military signaling
* The potential for a renewed Iran–Israel conflict
* Azerbaijan’s Middle East strategy and ties with Israel
* Europe’s fragmented and reactive geopolitical posture
* Armenia’s uncertain turn toward Europe amid regional instability

---

## **Key Questions Discussed**

* What does Washington’s move on Venezuelan airspace signal?
* How likely is a new Iran war, and what did the June fighting reveal?
* How does Azerbaijan’s partnership with Israel shape Iran’s threat perception?
* What role might Turkey or Azerbaijan play in a worst-case scenario inside Iran?
* What is Baku trying to achieve in the Middle East?
* Why is Europe resistant to diplomatic off-ramps in Ukraine?
* Can Europe act as an independent geopolitical force?
* What can Europe realistically offer Armenia in security terms?
* What posture should Armenia adopt amid rising regional risks?
